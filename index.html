The Distinction

Most families take trips.
Some families create legacies.

A beautiful destination. A stunning home. Everyone together, finally, in the same place at the same time. There is real joy in that — and we celebrate every moment of it.

But a Treasured Adventure is something different. It is a gathering designed around the person at its center — their craft, their stories, their way of being — and an intentional effort to capture what they carry so that it can be passed forward across every generation.

It is a celebration of a life richly lived. And it becomes the most extraordinary gift a family has ever given — or received.

"We came for the trip. We left with something we will keep forever."
Two Ways to Begin

Sometimes it's a gift.

The Gift They Will Never Forget

Planned by a child, a grandchild, or a devoted assistant

Her seventieth birthday is coming. Or their fiftieth anniversary. Or perhaps there is no occasion — only a quiet, persistent feeling that something important should be done while there is still time to do it beautifully.

You want to gather everyone. You want to capture who she is — her voice, her recipes, her stories, the way she makes everyone feel at home. You want him to know that the years he spent building, crafting, mentoring, creating were witnessed and will not be forgotten.

This can be planned entirely as a surprise. The gathering, the sessions, the film, the book — all of it designed and coordinated while the person at the center simply believes they are going somewhere beautiful with the people they love most.

The legacy package arrives at their door weeks later. That is when they understand what was truly given to them.

The Legacy You Choose to Leave

Designed by the grandparent, the patriarch, the matriarch

You have thought about it. Perhaps more than you have said aloud. The things you know. The way you do things. The recipes, the craft, the wisdom, the stories — the particular knowledge that lives in you and, so far, only in you.

You want your grandchildren to know you. Not the version of you that appears in photographs — the real version. The one at the workbench on a quiet Saturday morning. The one who knows exactly how much salt goes in without measuring. The one with stories worth telling.

A Treasured Adventure gives you the chance to design that experience yourself. To choose the setting, the theme, the people gathered around you. To decide what gets passed on, and how, and to whom.

This is not something that happens to you. It is something you create.

The Experience

Every moment is
thoughtfully designed.
None of them feel like it.

We hold the architecture so the family can simply be present. The schedule, the sessions, the conversation guides, the cameras that fade into the background — all of it invisible. What remains is the feeling of being together, unhurried, in a beautiful place, for the reasons that actually matter.

i.

The Gathering

A destination chosen for your family — not just beautiful, but right. A private residence with a kitchen worth cooking in, a workshop worth working in, grounds worth exploring. Excursions designed for every generation, from the youngest grandchild to the grandparent who prefers a long conversation on the terrace. Every logistic handled. Every preference anticipated.

Destination · Residence · Curation
ii.

The Signature Sessions

This is the heart of it. Intimate, unhurried time — grandchildren in the kitchen with their grandmother, learning the recipe carried for decades. Or beside their grandfather at the workbench. Or fishing at dawn. Or gathered around a piano. A small, discreet crew films these moments with warmth and extraordinary patience.

Sessions · Documentation · Craft
iii.

The Conversations

At thoughtfully chosen moments across the gathering, we sit with family members and ask them about the person at the center. What they remember. What they were taught without knowing they were being taught. What they want their own children to know. These interviews become the most treasured element of the final film.

Interviews · Stories · Voice
iv.

The Expert Touch

When the experience calls for it, we bring in someone who speaks the language of what your person loves. A master chef. A master woodworker. A musician, a historian, a sommelier, a master fly-fisherman. Someone whose presence elevates both the session and the understanding of what your person has spent a lifetime knowing.

Expertise · Depth · Discovery
v.

The Days Between

Adventure, rest, laughter, and long dinners. We design the in-between with equal care — because a life well-remembered is also a life well-celebrated. The mornings on the water. The afternoons in the garden. The teenagers who somehow put their phones down. The cousins who become real friends, perhaps for the first time.

Joy · Connection · Celebration
vi.

The Legacy Collection

The trip becomes a memory.
The collection becomes an heirloom.

Every Treasured Adventure produces a complete legacy collection — crafted with the same intention and care as the experience itself. These are not souvenirs. They are the permanent record of a family, designed to be held, to be opened on significant occasions, and passed to people who haven't been born yet.

People have filmed themselves opening the box — and sent us the video in tears. The packaging alone tells you, before you've seen a single page, that something important is inside.

The Legacy Film — a cinematic film, 30 to 45 minutes, weaving together the sessions, the interviews, the quiet moments, and the joy of the gathering. Scored, edited, produced to the standard of something you would choose to watch — again and again.
The Heirloom Book — a hardcover volume, professionally written and designed, containing stories, recipes, interviews, and photographs. Printed on archival paper. Bound to last. Multiple copies produced — one for every family — so the legacy lives in every home.
The Private Family Archive — a beautiful, secure, password-protected website housing the full archive: the film, the photographs, the recordings, and everything else that belongs there.
The Keepsake Presentation — ribbon, tissue, a handwritten note. The kind of unboxing that becomes its own memory.

How We Work

We work with a small number of families each year.
Not because we have to. Because we choose to.

This is the kind of work that requires full presence — full understanding of a single family's story. There are no templates here. No catalogue of options to select from. No version of this that is anything other than entirely, specifically yours.

Every engagement begins with a conversation — not a contract, not a proposal, not a questionnaire. A real conversation about the person at the center and what this experience might mean for your family.

We handle every detail. The destination, the residence, the staffing, the documentation, the production, the delivery. You handle nothing except being present with the people you love.

The investment in a Treasured Adventures experience reflects the depth of the customization, the caliber of the production, and the significance of what is created. We discuss this openly and without awkwardness in our first conversation.

i

The First Conversation

A real conversation — not a pitch. We want to understand who this person is, what they love, and what this gathering might mean. Nothing is signed. Nothing is decided. It begins here.

ii

The Experience Design

We design the entire journey from scratch — the destination, the residence, the schedule, the signature sessions, the expert invitations, the interview guides. Everything built around your family's story.

iii

The Gathering

We arrive before the family and become part of the background — present enough to capture every meaningful moment, invisible enough that it all feels entirely natural. Nothing is performed. Everything is real.

iv

The Production

Six to eight weeks of meticulous post-production — editing the film, writing and designing the heirloom book, building the private archive. This is where everything becomes something permanent.

v

The Unveiling

The package arrives — boxed, ribboned, heavier than expected. Inside is the film, the book, and the archive. Families have described this as the most significant gift they have ever received.
